Abstract

Soft robots have the advantage of safe interaction and dexterous operation in environments, since the multiple segments with soft material can bring flexibility and compliance compared to general rigid robots. However, due to its high-dimensional, nonlinear, time-varying, and infinite-degree-of-freedom characteristics, there would be challenges in achieving precise and dynamic control, like trajectory tracking or position reaching. In this paper, we propose a Deep Koopman-based Model Predictive Control (DK-MPC) method for multi-segment soft robots. Specifically, we first employ a deep learning approach with sampling data to approximate the Koopman operator, which allows us to linearize the high-dimensional nonlinear dynamics of the soft robot into a finite-dimensional linear representation. Then, we utilize this linearized model within a model predictive control framework to compute optimal control inputs that minimize the tracking error between the desired and actual trajectories. Real-world experiments on the soft robot “Chordata” demonstrate that DK-MPC can achieve can achieve high-precision control, which shows the potential of DK-MPC for future applications of soft robots.
